//Principals

Frank Pulcini

Frank is founder and President of Veros Advisors. He has 25 years of consulting experience advising Fortune 500 companies such as GlaxoSmithKline and Johnson & Johnson as well as many biotechnology and not-for-profit organizations. His consulting experience started in 1987 at The Vanderveer Group in custom pharmaceutical marketing research. From there he led two of his own healthcare marketing research consultancies for 20 years before founding Veros Advisors. He also held positions in management and retail marketing at Firestone Tire & Rubber Co and has 10 years of sales and sales training experience in the pharmaceutical industry at OJF, Sandoz and Ross Laboratories. Frank received a B.S. degree in Business Administration and Marketing from Villanova University’s School of Commerce & Finance.

Tom Downing

For 20 years, and from both sides of the client/consultant relationship, Tom has been discovering meaningful insights and helping translate them into strategy. Twelve of these years he spent at GlaxoSmithKline, including a stint in Tokyo with GSK Japan. As a consultant, he has advised pharmaceutical companies, device manufacturers and other healthcare organizations, as well as a wide range of consumer-facing organizations. He has taught anthropology at Gloucester County College in New Jersey, and has been a guest lecturer at Temple University. Tom is a past board member of the Pharmaceutical Marketing Research Group. He holds a degree in cultural anthropology from Brigham Young University and an MBA from the Marriott School of Management.

Matt Fieger

Matt holds a bachelor’s degree in physics from Colgate University. Matt contributes his problem solving background along with his passion for world affairs into Veros Advisors' framework.

Matt is adept at applying design principles in highly technical as well as strategic marketing projects. On the technical side, he worked on the design of radiochemical separation processes for accelerator mass spectrometry measurement on the part per trillion level. On the strategic end, Matt helped a major pharmaceutical industry client understand how large U.S hospitals and integrated delivery networks combat MRSA, leading to a strategic redesign of its brand educational approach. In a more recent project, he helped a pharmaceutical manufacturer redesign its institutional marketing program.

Matt has lived in Amman, Jordan and Damascus, Syria and has studied at both University of Jordan and Damascus University. He enjoys international travel and studies Arabic in his free time.

//Advisor Team

Richard J. Anthony, Sr.

Rick has over 35 years of experience as a manager and counselor to senior management. His book, Organizations, People & Effective Communication, was published in 2008 by University Readers Press.

He has extensive consulting experience with clients in the private, public and not-for-profit sectors. Much of his consulting involves diagnostic analysis, coaching, teambuilding and working with groups of managers to establish clarity and priorities around strategic and operational issues.

Rick began his career as a teacher and as a creative writer in advertising and public relations. Prior to founding SOLUTIONS, he had been Chairman and CEO of the Alexander Consulting Group’s U.S. consulting unit, where he established the firm’s Global Organizational Effectiveness consulting practice, and Vice President and Principal of Towers Perrin, one of the world’s largest human resource management consulting firms.

He is a member of the adjunct faculty at Villanova University and is Entrepreneur-in-Residence at the university's Center for Innovation, Creativity and Entrepreneurship.

Dan Copeland

Dan started on the industry side of market research with a large pharmaceutical manufacturer in the early 2000s. Today, he is President of Emerge Life Sciences, a qualitative market research firm focused on emerging products in the pharmaceutical, biological and medical device arena.

Dan’s research experience includes a large number of exploratory initiatives for new products and evaluating physician and patient reaction to new and emerging data. Dan has also led global research efforts to position and/or reposition compounds in the U.S. and Europe and explore medical decision making among treaters and patients throughout the world. He received his MBA from The University of North Carolina Kenan-Flagler Business School in 1998.

When he is not wading through the details of a research project, Dan likes to spend his time knee deep in a trout stream in northern Colorado or watching his kids play baseball in Chapel Hill, NC.

Beth Carey Fuller

Beth is the Director of Global Logistics for Veros Advisors. She has spent over 10 years cultivating relationships all over the world to ensure that there is a vast wealth of resources for every type of project imaginable. Beth has managed logistics for companies big and small across a wide variety of industries.

Beth has been very active in the Market Researchers Association. From 2007-2011 Beth has served on the board of the Philadelphia Chapter of the MRA; she signed on as Secretary and later was elected President.

Ken Leahy

Ken’s career includes thirty years with the Johnson & Johnson Family of Companies. His responsibilities increased from the financial management sector of pharmaceuticals, medical devices and industrial products to top leadership as the Chief Financial Officer and VP of Research Services for R.W. Johnson Pharmaceutical Research Institute.

Ken has had several international assignments in Canada, Europe and Asia in both major financial and personnel supervisory roles. He has served as a board member of more than one J&J company.

Ken is adept in helping organizations design their business model and corporate strategy. He also serves on several not-for-profit boards.

Ron Ferner

In addition to his work at Veros Advisors, Ron is Dean of the School of Business and Leadership, and Chair of the Undergraduate Business Administration program at Philadelphia Biblical University.

Ron has many years of executive experience at Campbell’s. During his career at Campbell’s, Ron held plant manager positions at several Campbell plants including Fremont, Nebraska; Worthington, Minnesota; and Chicago, Illinois. Ron also held management assignments at Campbell’s World Headquarters in Camden, New Jersey including Vice President – Operations/Logistics Strategy. He was transferred to Sacramento, California as Vice President – Manufacturing, Western Region. His last assignment was at Campbell’s World Headquarters in Camden, New Jersey as Vice President Low Cost Business Systems responsible for improving manufacturing and customer service processes throughout the company.

Ron received a Bachelor of Science degree in Food Engineering from the Illinois Institute of Technology, and an M.B.A. from the University of Chicago.

Fred W. Thomas

Fred’s career includes over 34 years of varied experience in market research, business intelligence, business development, product development, marketing, sales, and management. He recently retired from Celgene Corporation where he built and managed the market research and business intelligence functions, and also worked in project management and government relations/public policy.

Fred holds pharmacy and law degrees from Temple University and a Master of Public Administration degree from Penn State University. He volunteers with several non-profit organizations, and enjoys his time with his family and traveling.

Bob Johnstone

Bob Johnstone is a career aeronautical engineer (BS, Penn State) with much of his career working in the US Navy on top security national defense projects. For the last 15 years of his career he served in upper management focusing on the development of US advanced weapon systems for sensitive projects. While living in Washington, DC he developed important international connections.

Bob is a founding member of IBEC Ventures and continues to bring a wealth of business leadership, drawn not only from industry but also from many years working for various NGOs and international agencies where he pursued strategic leadership training and mentoring.

Bob Rabner

As a founding partner of the first computer based graphic design firm in Philadelphia in 1987 Bob Rabner endeavors to serve his clients through the seamless integration of state of the art design technology and good, old fashioned creativity. The result continues to be the delivery of real world solutions to a diverse roster of design clients. Bob brings the same level of passion and creative enthusiasm to every project whether working with a Fortune 500 Company or an entrepreneurial startup.

Bob Meiers

Bob is a founding partner and President of GM&P Strategic Advertising. He has over 30 years experience designing and implementing strategic marketing plans, resulting in increase of market share and sales for his clients. Bob is unrelenting in his pursuit of understanding the underlying motives for consumer purchase behavior of his client’s products and services.

Some highlights of his career are setting the opening week sales record for an individual McDonald’s Restaurant, repositioning Friendly’s Restaurant and redesigning the Lasik Eye Surgery Industry.
